Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Are nose strips good for oily skin?

Ultimately, while satisfying and instantly gratifying, pore strips aren't an effective long-term solution for blackheads and skin texture. However, they are okay to use in moderation if you have an oily skin type or need a quick fix for clear-looking skin.

2. Do pore strips make your pores bigger?

Increased Pore Size: False However, according to Dr. Tanzi, there simply isn't any proof that pore strips can make your pores larger. “Pore size gets worse with age and sun exposure or conditions like rosacea, which run in families,” she explains, “the size of pores is based on genetics, too.”

3. Do pore strips damage pores?

Can Pore Strips Cause Damage? Pore strips are unlikely to cause any real damage. However, they may cause redness and skin irritation, especially for those with skin disorders like rosacea or psoriasis, or for patients using prescription acne medications.

4. Do dermatologists recommend pore strips?

The Final Takeaway. While pore strips are a fun and satisfying way to unclog your pores, the dermatologists we consulted are not convinced they're worth the possible damage they can cause to the skin. Stick with other, safer–albeit slightly less exciting–methods when deep cleaning your pores for better results.
