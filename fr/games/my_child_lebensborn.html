Game Introduction

1. Introduction of the game:
"My Child Lebensborn" is a poignant and thought-provoking story-driven simulation game that invites players into the post-World War II era to raise an adopted child. As the player, you become the guardian of a young Lebensborn child in Norway, experiencing the complexities and challenges of nurturing a young life in an environment marred by the lingering hatred towards Nazis and their offspring. The game skillfully blends elements of caregiving with narrative choices, influencing the growth and development of the child under your care.

2. All characters and levels of the game:
The main character is the child you choose to adopt, either Klaus or Karin, whose story you follow throughout the game. There are no traditional levels as you might find in other games; instead, the game progresses through days and weeks, marking the child's development stages and key plot points. Supporting characters include neighbors, schoolteachers, and other children, each playing a role in your child's life and contributing to the evolving narrative.

3. What attracts users most to the game:
Users are drawn to "My Child Lebensborn" for its emotional depth, unique historical context, and moral dilemmas. The game's ability to evoke empathy and its educational value in presenting historical events from a personal perspective are significant draws. The nurturing aspect of the game is also attractive, as players form a bond with their adopted child and make impactful decisions.

4. Why it is popular:
The popularity of "My Child Lebensborn" is due in part to its rich storytelling and the emotional connection it fosters. It addresses complex issues such as identity, prejudice, and the consequences of war, offering a unique gaming experience that is both enlightening and emotionally resonant. By combining the mechanics of a life simulation game with a significant historical backdrop, it stands out in a crowded gaming landscape.

5. The most difficult part of the game:
The most challenging aspect of "My Child Lebensborn" is managing the emotional well-being of your child amidst the societal challenges they face. Decisions made by the player can have significant effects on the child's mood, school performance, and social interactions, requiring careful consideration and sometimes facing heart-wrenching outcomes.

6. Strengths and weaknesses of the game:
Strengths of the game include its powerful narrative, educational value, and the emotional investment it demands of players. The art style and sound design also contribute to an immersive experience. However, some players might find the pace slow, as the game focuses more on storytelling than on action. Additionally, the heavy themes may not be suitable for all audiences.

7. What other games have been developed by the same developer:
"My Child Lebensborn" was developed by Sarepta Studio in collaboration with Teknopilot. Sarepta Studio has also worked on other games such as "Shadow Puppeteer," an adventure puzzle-platformer. The collaboration with Teknopilot is especially notable with "My Child Lebensborn" for its focus on narrative and historically inspired storytelling.
